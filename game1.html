Extreme Temperatures
The Earth's average temperature has increased by about 2°F (1°C) since the late 1800s. This rise is mostly due to more carbon dioxide and other gases being released into the air by human activities like burning fossil fuels. These gases trap heat, causing the planet to warm up.










Results :
Hotter Weather: As the Earth gets warmer, we experience more heatwaves, making it harder to stay cool and safe.
Melting Ice: The heat is causing ice at the North and South Poles to melt, which makes the sea levels rise and can lead to flooding.
Hurt Animals and Plants: Some animals and plants can't survive in the hotter weather, which could lead to fewer species and damaged ecosystems.
