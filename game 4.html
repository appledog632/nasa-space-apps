Ocean ACIDIFICATION
Warming Waters
Weaker Shells: Sea creatures like clams, oysters, and snails have trouble building strong shells, making it harder for them to survive.
Harmed Coral Reefs: Coral reefs, which are home to many sea animals, get damaged and can start dying off.
Less Food for Fish: As some sea creatures struggle, fish and other animals that eat them may have less food, affecting the whole ocean food chain.
Ocean acidification happens when the ocean absorbs too much carbon dioxide from the air, making the water more acidic. This can harm sea animals like coral, shellfish, and some fish, because it makes it harder for them to grow strong shells or survive. It also affects the food chain, which can impact other sea creatures and even humans who rely on the ocean for food
