Glacier Retreat
FRESHWATER IMPACT
Glacier retreat happens when glaciers, which are huge sheets of ice, start to melt and shrink. As they melt, they get smaller and pull back, leaving less ice on mountains. This can cause rivers to dry up and less water for animals and people who depend on it. It also makes sea levels rise, which can lead to flooding in some places











Results :
Less Water: Melting glaciers can reduce the amount of water available in rivers and streams, affecting people, animals, and plants.
Sea Level Rise: As glaciers melt, the extra water flows into the oceans, causing sea levels to rise, which can lead to flooding in coastal areas.
Changing Landscapes: Mountains and valleys where glaciers used to be can become bare and rocky, changing the environment for the plants and animals that live there.
