Flooding: As sea levels rise, coastal areas can flood, damaging homes, schools, and roads.
Disappearing Land: Some beaches and islands are slowly being covered by water, which reduces space for people and animals to live.
Harm to Marine Life: Rising tides can change the water's salt levels, affecting fish and other sea creatures that need specific conditions to survive.
Rising tides happen when the sea level gets higher, causing more water to cover the land. This can lead to flooding, where water can damage homes and roads. Some beaches and islands might disappear underwater, and sea animals can have trouble living in the changing water.
